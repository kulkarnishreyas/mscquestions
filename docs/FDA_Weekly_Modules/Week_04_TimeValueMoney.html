1️⃣ Present Value

Time Value of Money (TVM): ₹1 today is worth more than ₹1 tomorrow because today's money can earn returns.

PV = FV / (1 + r)ⁿ
Example: Receive ₹1,000 in 5 years at 5% discount rate.
PV = 1000 / (1.05)⁵ ≈ ₹783.53 today.

Discount Rate Types

🛡️ Risk-Free Rate

Govt bond yields (~4% for 10-yr)

🏢 WACC

Weighted Avg Cost of Capital — corporate hurdle rate

📈 Market-Based

Investor expectations (20–30% for VC)

💸 Inflation-Adjusted

Real rate = Nominal − Inflation

Impact of Rate Changes

Discount RatePV of ₹15,000 in 8 yrs
3%₹11,850
5%₹10,151
7%₹8,737

Higher discount rate → lower PV.

Applications

  • Investment valuation, NPV analysis
  • Loan structuring, lease payments
  • Pension fund obligations
  • Capital budgeting (project selection)
  • Insurance premium calculation

2️⃣ Future Value

FV = PV × (1 + r)ⁿ   |   FV = PV × (1 + r/m)ⁿᵐ (for m compoundings/yr)
Example: ₹3,000 at 5% for 7 years annually.
FV = 3000 × (1.05)⁷ ≈ ₹4,226.46

Compounding Frequency Effect

₹5,000 at 6% for 5 years:

  • Annual: 5000 × (1.06)⁵ = ₹6,691
  • Quarterly: 5000 × (1 + 0.06/4)²⁰ = ₹6,744
  • Monthly: 5000 × (1 + 0.06/12)⁶⁰ ≈ ₹6,747

More frequent compounding → higher FV.

Inflation Adjustment (Real FV)

FV_real = FV_nominal / (1 + i)ⁿ
₹10,000 grows at 7% for 10 yrs → nominal ₹19,672. With 3% inflation → real ₹14,665.

FV of Annuity (Periodic Deposits)

FV_annuity = P × [(1 + r)ⁿ − 1] / r
₹2,000/month at 8% for 10 years (n=120, r=0.08/12) → FV ≈ ₹3,64,573. Small regular saves compound massively.

3️⃣ Annuity & Perpetuity

Annuity

Series of equal payments at regular intervals for a fixed period.

🏠 Ordinary Annuity

Payments at end of period (loan EMIs)

📅 Annuity Due

Payments at beginning (rent, insurance)

📊 Variable Annuity

Payments fluctuate with underlying investment

Perpetuity

Equal payments forever. PV = Payment / r.

Example: Preferred stock paying ₹500/yr at 10% discount rate.
PV = 500/0.10 = ₹5,000

Growing Perpetuity

PV = P / (r − g)   where g = growth rate

Annuity vs Perpetuity

FeatureAnnuityPerpetuity
DurationFixed periodIndefinite
End dateYesNo
Valuationn, r, PP, r only
ExamplesMortgages, retirement annuitiesConsol bonds, preferred stock dividends

Applications

  • Retirement planning, structured income
  • Pension funds, education savings
  • Mortgages and loan repayments
  • Charitable endowments, royalties, infrastructure tolls

4️⃣ Loan Amortization & Compounding

Amortization: repaying loan via regular payments. Each EMI covers interest + principal.
Over time: interest portion shrinks, principal portion grows.

Simple vs Compound Interest

TypeFormula
SimpleSI = P × r × t
CompoundCI = P × (1 + r/n)ⁿᵗ

Compounding Frequency Effect

₹1,00,000 at 10% for 1 year:

  • Annually → ₹1,10,000
  • Quarterly → ₹1,10,381
  • Monthly → ₹1,10,471

Repayment Structures

💳 EMI (Fixed)

Same monthly payment throughout. Used in home/car loans.

🎈 Balloon Payment

Small periodic + large final lump sum.

⏩ Early Repayment

Extra payments cut total interest, shorten tenure.

Applications

  • Mortgages — amortisation schedule shows interest/principal split
  • Investment vs Loans — compare return vs cost of capital
  • Business Financing — predictable cash outflows for asset purchases

5️⃣ Markowitz Portfolio Theory (MPT)

Harry Markowitz (1952): diversification across uncorrelated assets reduces risk without sacrificing return.

Core Principle: Risk-Return Trade-off

Two Types of Risk

🌐 Systematic Risk

Market-wide (interest rates, politics). Cannot be diversified.

🏢 Unsystematic Risk

Asset-specific (company issues). Can be diversified away.

Mean-Variance Optimization (Formulas)

E(Rp) = Σ wᵢ E(Rᵢ)   |   σp² = Σᵢ Σⱼ wᵢ wⱼ σᵢⱼ

Steps

  1. Estimate expected returns and risks (using historical data)
  2. Calculate covariance/correlation matrix
  3. Optimise weights to maximise return at given risk (or minimise risk for given return)
  4. Identify optimal point on efficient frontier per risk tolerance

MPT Assumptions & Limitations

AssumptionReality Check
Rational investorsBehavioural biases exist
Normally distributed returnsReal returns have fat tails
Stable correlationsCorrelations spike during crises
Market efficiencyInefficiencies exist
Single-period horizonIgnores long-term compounding

Risk-Adjusted Metrics

  • Sharpe Ratio = excess return per total risk unit
  • Treynor Ratio = excess return per market risk (beta)

6️⃣ Hands-On in Excel

Key Excel Functions for Portfolio

FunctionPurpose
=SUMPRODUCT(weights, returns)Expected portfolio return
=STDEV.S(range)Historical volatility
=COVARIANCE.S(r1, r2)Covariance between assets
=CORREL(r1, r2)Correlation
=MMULT(...)Matrix multiplication for portfolio variance

Solver Optimization Steps

  1. Objective: maximise expected return OR minimise risk
  2. Variable cells: asset weights
  3. Constraints: weights sum to 1, weights ≥ 0 (no short)
  4. Run Solver from Data tab
  5. Compare optimised weights, return, SD, Sharpe

Building the Efficient Frontier

  1. Set up returns & covariance matrix
  2. Generate many weight combinations (random or grid)
  3. Calculate portfolio return & risk for each
  4. Plot scatter: X = SD, Y = Return → curved upper envelope = Efficient Frontier

Risk-Return Trade-off Metrics

  • Sharpe Ratio: (Rp − Rf) / σp
  • Sortino Ratio: uses downside deviation only — better when returns are skewed
  • Beta: sensitivity to overall market

Correlation Heatmap for Diversification

Use Excel's Conditional Formatting → Color Scale on a correlation matrix. Aim to combine assets with low (< 0.5) or negative correlation for diversification benefits.

📝 Summary & Exam Tips

🎯 Key Takeaways

  • TVM: ₹1 today > ₹1 future. PV = FV/(1+r)ⁿ; FV = PV(1+r)ⁿ
  • More frequent compounding → higher FV; higher discount rate → lower PV
  • Annuity: fixed payments, fixed period. Perpetuity: forever (PV = P/r)
  • Loan EMI: interest decreases, principal increases over time
  • MPT: diversification reduces unsystematic risk. Efficient Frontier = optimal portfolios
  • Risk types: systematic (can't diversify), unsystematic (can)
  • Risk-adjusted metrics: Sharpe (total risk), Treynor (beta), Sortino (downside only)
  • Excel Solver optimises portfolio weights given return/risk constraints
  • Combine assets with low/negative correlation for max diversification benefit
📌 Pitfalls:
  • Confusing nominal vs real returns (always check inflation adjustment)
  • Forgetting MPT assumes normal returns — fails in crashes (fat tails)
  • Treating correlations as static — they spike during market stress
  • Excessive diversification dilutes high performers
