1️⃣ Financial Statements Overview

Financial statements are tools that show an organisation's financial health. They guide decisions on investments, operations, and compliance.

The Three Core Statements

How They Interrelate

Income → Balance Sheet

Net Income flows into Retained Earnings (equity section). Profitable year = equity grows.

Balance Sheet ↔ Cash Flow

Changes in receivables, payables, PPE all appear in cash flow categories.

Income → Cash Flow

CFO starts with Net Income, adjusts for non-cash items (depreciation) and working capital changes.

📋 Tech Solutions Ltd. (example):
  • Revenue ₹5,00,000 − COGS ₹3,00,000 − OpEx ₹1,00,000 = Net Income ₹1,00,000
  • Net Profit Margin = 100,000/500,000 = 20%
  • Total Assets ₹7,00,000; Liabilities ₹4,00,000; Equity ₹3,00,000
  • D/E Ratio = 4,00,000/3,00,000 = 1.33
  • CFO +₹1,20,000; CFI −₹80,000; CFF +₹50,000 → Net Cash +₹90,000

2️⃣ Balance Sheet Analysis

Assets = Liabilities + Equity

Components

📦 Assets

Current (≤1 yr — cash, receivables, inventory) and Non-Current (PPE, intangibles).

💳 Liabilities

Current (payables, short-term loans) and Non-Current (long-term debt).

👥 Equity

Residual: Assets − Liabilities. Includes share capital, retained earnings.

Key Balance Sheet Ratios

Equity RatioTotal Equity / Total AssetsHigher = less debt dependence

📋 Case Study: XYZ Corporation

Given: Current Assets ₹1,70,000 (incl. inventory ₹40,000); Non-Current ₹2,30,000. Current Liab ₹90,000; Non-Current Liab ₹1,50,000. Equity ₹1,60,000. Net Sales ₹5,00,000; Net Income ₹40,000.

  • Current Ratio = 170/90 = 1.89 (strong)
  • Quick Ratio = (170−40)/90 = 1.44
  • D/E = 240/160 = 1.5
  • Debt Ratio = 240/400 = 0.6 (60% debt-financed)
  • Asset Turnover = 500/400 = 1.25
  • ROA = 40/400 × 100 = 10%
  • ROE = 40/160 × 100 = 25%
  • Working Capital = 170 − 90 = ₹80,000

Liquidity, Solvency, & Leverage

💧 Liquidity

Ability to meet short-term obligations. Measured via Current & Quick ratios.

🏛️ Solvency

Ability to meet long-term obligations. D/E and Debt ratios.

📊 Leverage

How much debt is used. Higher leverage = higher risk + higher potential return.

📋 Example: Revenue ₹40,00,000, COGS ₹25,00,000, OpEx ₹7,00,000, Tax ₹3,00,000.
  • Gross Profit = 40 − 25 = ₹15 lakh → Gross Margin = 37.5%
  • Operating Income = 15 − 7 = ₹8 lakh → Op Margin = 20%
  • Net Income = 8 − 3 = ₹5 lakh → Net Margin = 12.5%

Growth Rates

Growth Rate = (Current − Previous) / Previous × 100
  • Revenue Growth: 45L vs 40L → +12.5%
  • Net Income Growth: 6L vs 5L → +20%
  • EPS Growth: tracks per-share profitability

4️⃣ Cash Flow Statement Analysis

The Three Sections

⚙️ CFO — Operating

Cash from core business. Starts with net income, adjusts non-cash items (depreciation) and working-capital changes. Positive = healthy ops.

🏗️ CFI — Investing

Cash for buying/selling long-term assets. Negative often = growth investment; positive may signal asset sales.

💰 CFF — Financing

Cash from debt & equity. Positive = raising funds; negative = repaying debt or returning to shareholders.

Free Cash Flow (FCF) = CFO − Capital Expenditures

Cash Flow Ratios

RatioFormulaUse
OCF RatioCFO / Current LiabilitiesShort-term liquidity from operations
Cash Flow to DebtCFO / Total DebtDebt servicing ability
Cash Flow CoverageCFO / (Interest + Principal)Debt repayment buffer
Cash Flow MarginCFO / RevenueCash efficiency from sales
FCF / OCFFCF / CFO% of cash free for distribution
💡 Profit ≠ Cash: A profitable company can still go bankrupt if it can't collect receivables (lots of profit on paper, no actual cash). The Cash Flow Statement reveals this hidden truth.

5️⃣ Financial Ratios & Ratio Analysis

4 Categories of Ratios

🔵 Liquidity Ratios (short-term obligations)

  • Current Ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities (target > 1)
  • Quick Ratio = (Current Assets − Inventory) / Current Liabilities (target > 0.7)

🟢 Profitability Ratios

  • Gross Profit Margin, Operating Margin, Net Profit Margin
  • ROA = Net Income / Total Assets × 100
  • ROE = Net Income / Equity × 100

🔴 Leverage Ratios

  • D/E Ratio = Total Liab / Equity
  • Interest Coverage = Operating Income / Interest Expense (higher = safer)

🟠 Efficiency Ratios

  • Asset Turnover = Revenue / Total Assets
  • Inventory Turnover = COGS / Average Inventory

Interpreting Trends

📈 Profitability Trends

Rising gross/operating/net margins = stronger pricing, cost control.

💧 Liquidity Trends

Improving = better cash management; declining = potential distress.

🏛️ Solvency Trends

Falling D/E = reduced debt reliance; rising interest coverage = stability.

⚙️ Efficiency Trends

Rising asset turnover = better resource use.

6️⃣ Data Visualisation Techniques

Python: Pie Chart (Market Share)

import matplotlib.pyplot as plt companies = ['Company A','Company B','Company C','Company D'] share = [30, 25, 20, 25] plt.pie(share, labels=companies, autopct='%1.1f%%', startangle=90) plt.title('Market Share of Companies') plt.show()

Python: Line Chart (Monthly Revenue)

months = ['Jan','Feb','Mar','Apr'] revenue = [50000, 52000, 51000, 53000] plt.plot(months, revenue, marker='o') plt.title('Monthly Revenue Trend') plt.xlabel('Month'); plt.ylabel('Revenue (₹)') plt.show()

R: Bar Chart (ggplot2)

library(ggplot2) data <- data.frame(Quarter=c('Q1','Q2','Q3','Q4'), Profit=c(20000,25000,23000,27000)) ggplot(data, aes(x=Quarter, y=Profit)) + geom_bar(stat='identity', fill='blue') + ggtitle('Quarterly Profit')

Excel Options

  • Pivot Tables & Charts for aggregating large datasets
  • Built-in Charts: Line, Bar, Column, Pie
  • Conditional Formatting for heatmap-style highlighting

Design Principles

  • Simplify — remove decoration, no 3D effects
  • Right chart for the data — don't use pie for >6 categories
  • Clear labels — titles, axis labels, legends
  • Colour wisely — accessibility, high-contrast
  • Truthful scaling — start y-axis at 0 when comparing magnitudes
  • Add context — annotations, benchmarks

📝 Summary & Exam Tips

🎯 Key Takeaways

  • 3 statements: Income (period), Balance Sheet (point), Cash Flow (period)
  • Equation: Assets = Liabilities + Equity
  • Income Statement waterfall: Revenue → Gross Profit → Op Income → Pre-tax → Net Income
  • Cash Flow sections: CFO + CFI + CFF = Net Cash Change
  • FCF = CFO − CapEx; the cash available for distribution / debt paydown
  • Ratios: Liquidity (Current, Quick), Profitability (GP%, OP%, NP%, ROA, ROE), Leverage (D/E, Interest Coverage), Efficiency (Asset Turnover)
  • Statements interconnect: Net income → Retained Earnings; Receivables change → CFO
  • Viz: line for trends, bar for categories, pie for proportions, scatter for relationships, heatmap for correlations
📌 Common Exam Pitfalls:
  • Forgetting that Income Statement is for a period, Balance Sheet at a point
  • Confusing CFO with Net Income (CFO adjusts for non-cash items)
  • Positive CFI ≠ good (could mean asset sales / distress)
  • D/E uses total liabilities, not just long-term debt
  • ROE uses average equity, not end-period
