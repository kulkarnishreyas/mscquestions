⭐ Compulsory / High-Priority Topics

The professor explicitly called these compulsory or "very, very important." Prepare these thoroughly first.

Reinforcement Learning Compulsory

  • How RL works — the agent ⇄ environment loop (state, action, reward).
  • The benefits and the core idea of learning from rewards (no labels).
  • The different types of RL.
Likely Q: "Explain how reinforcement learning works, its benefits, and the different types of RL." Write as clear steps + a diagram.
📘 Week 7 — Reinforcement Learning

Stacking & Ensemble Learning Compulsory

  • How stacking works — base learners feed a meta-model (2-layer model).
  • How stacking differs from bagging and boosting.
  • Pros and cons of bagging, boosting, and stacking.
  • Boosting & bagging may also appear as slightly smaller questions.
Likely Q: "Explain how stacking works and how it differs from other ensemble techniques," and/or "Give the pros and cons of bagging, boosting and stacking."
📘 Week 12 — Bagging, Boosting & Stacking · Week 11 — Ensembles & Voting

Bayes' Theorem & Discriminant Functions Compulsory

  • Classify which class an event belongs to using Bayes' theorem.
  • Use discriminant functions to find the decision boundary via Bayes.
P(h|D) = P(D|h)·P(h) / P(D)
Likely Q: "Using Bayes' theorem, classify the given event," or "How is the decision boundary found using a discriminant function?"
📘 Week 5 — Bayesian Learning & Naive Bayes

Confusion Matrix → Accuracy / Precision / Recall / F1 Very Important

  • Called "very, very important" — expect MCQs and a big question.
  • Also a likely numerical: given a confusion matrix, compute the metrics.
Accuracy = (TP+TN)/Total  |  Precision = TP/(TP+FP)  |  Recall = TP/(TP+FN)  |  F1 = 2·P·R/(P+R)
📘 Week 2 — Evaluation Metrics · 🧮 Practice

Perceptron / Neural-Network Output Guaranteed Numerical

  • Given inputs X1, X2, weights, and an activation (sigmoid / ReLU / step), compute the neuron's output.
  • Know how the perceptron updates its weights.
z = Σ wᵢxᵢ + b  →  output = f(z)   (e.g. sigmoid 1/(1+e⁻ᶻ))

Strongly recommended — likely to appear in Part C and across the MCQs.

CNN vs RNN vs Transformers & Transfer Learning Emphasized

  • General architecture of CNN, RNN, and transformer-based models.
  • When to use CNN vs RNN vs transformer; what transfer learning is.
📘 Week 10 — CNN, Transfer Learning & RNNs · Week 9 — Deep Learning

SVM — Working Principle & Kernel Strategy Emphasized

  • Explain how SVM works and the kernel trick (steps + diagram where possible).
📘 Week 3 — SVM · Week 4 — Kernel SVM

KNN vs K-Means Clustering Emphasized

  • KNN = supervised (nearest-neighbour class label); K-Means = unsupervised clustering. Know both working principles.
📘 Week 1 — KNN · Week 6 — K-Means Clustering

Regression Performance Metrics Emphasized

  • For logistic & linear regression: assess with R², MSE, MAE — know how to compute each.
R² = 1 − RSS/TSS  |  MSE = (1/n)Σ(y−ŷ)²  |  MAE = (1/n)Σ|y−ŷ|

✅ How to Attempt the Paper (the professor's advice)

Attempt every question. Write all the long-answer questions (whether 4 or 6) — don't leave any blank. Grace marks are only possible if you write reasonable, relevant content.
Stay relevant. Irrelevant answers earn no grace marks — answer exactly what is asked.
5-mark questions: point-wise answers and comparison tables are fine. For "working principle" questions, write the steps and draw a diagram wherever possible (CNN, RNN, RL, KNN, SVM, etc.).
The uploaded LMS study material is "more than enough" to clear the exam — go through all of it, especially for the MCQs.

🚫 What NOT to Do

Don't study from the mid-term / CAT / digital-assignment papers. The professor said that pattern was framed for those evaluations, not the FAT — his FAT question style is different now.
Don't rely on the earlier "sample FAT 2026" PDF as the pattern. His mindset has changed since sharing it; some of its numericals look complex, but he is not asking anything that complex this semester. Follow this revision-class guidance instead.
Don't over-prepare complex numericals. Keep numerical practice to the simple types listed above.
