1. What is Marketing Analytics?

Marketing Analytics is the practice of measuring, managing and analysing marketing performance data to maximise its effectiveness and optimise return on investment. It uses statistics, data science and business models to understand customer behaviour and guide decisions.

4. RFM Analysis

RFM is a customer segmentation technique based on purchasing behaviour. It scores each customer on three dimensions:

Fig 1.7 – RFM Cube: each dimension scored 1–4 (4 = most favourable)
Recency Score: 4 = purchased within last 7 days; 1 = purchased 90+ days ago
Frequency Score: 4 = 10+ purchases; 1 = only 1 purchase
Monetary Score: 4 = top 25% spender; 1 = bottom 25% spender
RFM Score = concatenation of R, F, M scores (e.g., 4-4-4 = Champion)

Hypothesis Testing Framework

  1. State H₀ (null: no effect) and H₁ (alternative: there is an effect)
  2. Choose significance level α = 0.05 (5% chance of Type I error)
  3. Select the appropriate test (z-test, t-test, ANOVA, chi-square)
  4. Calculate test statistic and corresponding p-value
  5. Decision: if p < α, reject H₀ → result is statistically significant
p-value: Probability of observing the result (or more extreme) IF H₀ is true
Reject H₀ when p-value < α (typically 0.05)
Type I Error (α): Rejecting a TRUE null hypothesis → False Positive
Type II Error (β): Failing to reject a FALSE null hypothesis → False Negative
Power = 1 − β (probability of correctly detecting a real effect)
Exam Mnemonic: Error Types

Type I = "Cry Wolf" — You alarm people about nothing (false positive, you reject H₀ when it was true)

Type II = "Miss the Wolf" — The wolf was real but you ignored it (false negative, you failed to reject H₀ when it was false)

7. A/B Testing & Marketing Mix Modelling

A/B Testing

A/B testing (split testing) is a controlled experiment where two variants are compared on a target metric to determine which performs better.

Worked Example – Email Campaign
GroupnConversionsRate
A (Control) – Original subject line5,0002505.0%
B (Treatment) – New subject line5,0003256.5%

Lift = (6.5% − 5.0%) / 5.0% = 30% relative improvement

Run a two-proportion z-test: H₀: p_A = p_B; H₁: p_B > p_A

If calculated z > 1.645 (one-tailed) or p-value < 0.05 → Significant! Adopt variant B.

z = (p̂_B − p̂_A) / √[p̂(1−p̂)(1/n_A + 1/n_B)]
where p̂ = (x_A + x_B) / (n_A + n_B) is the pooled proportion

A/B Testing Checklist

  • Randomly assign users to ensure groups are comparable
  • Test one variable at a time (isolate the effect)
  • Run until minimum sample size is reached (power analysis)
  • Watch for novelty effect and Simpson's paradox
  • Report absolute AND relative lift

Marketing Mix Modelling (MMM)

MMM uses regression to decompose sales into contributions from each marketing channel while controlling for price, seasonality and external factors.

Sales = β₀ + β₁(TV_spend) + β₂(Digital_spend) + β₃(Price) + β₄(Seasonality) + ε

β₀ = baseline sales (without any marketing)
β₁, β₂ = revenue generated per ₹ of TV/Digital spend
β₃ = price elasticity effect
ε = unexplained residual
MMM Interpretation Example

Fitted model: Sales = 120,000 + 4.2(TV) + 6.8(Digital) − 3,500(Price) + ε

→ Each ₹1 of Digital spend generates ₹6.80 in sales (ROMI = 5.8x)

→ Each ₹1 of TV spend generates ₹4.20 in sales (ROMI = 3.2x)

→ A ₹1 price increase reduces sales by ₹3,500 (elastic demand)

Decision: Reallocate budget from TV to Digital for better ROI

9. Data Privacy & Ethics

Compliance with data privacy regulations is crucial when collecting and analysing customer data. Ethical data usage helps build customer trust and safeguards organisations from legal issues.

Key Regulations

  • GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) — European Union rules covering consent, data minimisation, the right to be forgotten, and breach notification.
  • CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act) — gives California consumers rights to know what personal data is collected, request deletion, and opt out of sale of data.

Data-Driven Marketing & Ethical Use

  • Marketing analytics drives data-driven decision-making — but with that power comes responsibility for how data is gathered and used.
  • Customer trust is built by clear consent, transparency, and lawful processing.
  • Non-compliance can result in heavy fines and reputational damage.

Summary & Exam Tips

4 Analytics Levels

Descriptive → Diagnostic → Predictive → Prescriptive. Know one example for each.

7Ps vs 4Ps

Original 4Ps: Product, Price, Place, Promotion. Added 3: People, Process, Physical Evidence.

RFM Scoring

Score 1–4 per dimension. 4=best for all. 4-4-4 = Champion. Low recency score = churned.

Porter's Five Forces

New Entrants, Suppliers, Buyers, Substitutes, Rivalry. NOT SWOT — don't confuse!

Error Types

Type I = false positive (cry wolf). Type II = false negative (miss the wolf). Power = 1−β.

Attribution Models

5 models: First, Last, Linear, U-shaped (40/20/40), Time Decay. Know the split for U-shaped.

Ansoff Matrix

2×2: existing/new markets vs existing/new products. Diversification = highest risk.

MMM Formula

Sales = β₀ + β₁(TV) + β₂(Digital) + β₃(Price) + ε. β₀ = baseline without marketing.

Common Exam Pitfalls
  • Confusing Prescriptive (what to do) with Predictive (what will happen)
  • Forgetting the 3 extended Ps — exam often asks about "7Ps vs 4Ps"
  • Mixing up Type I error (α, false positive) and Type II error (β, false negative)
  • Applying U-shaped model incorrectly — middle touchpoints share only 20% collectively
  • Confusing Market Development (existing product, new market) with Market Penetration
  • RFM: higher recency score = MORE recent purchase (4 = last week, not last year)

Fresh exam-style numerical problems built from this week's formulas and frameworks. Enter your answer and press Check for instant feedback, or reveal the full worked solution. (Press Enter in any answer box to check.)
NP1In RFM analysis a customer is scored on a 1–5 scale: Recency = 4, Frequency = 3, Monetary = 5. The combined RFM score is formed by concatenating the three digits (R, then F, then M). What 3-digit RFM code does this customer receive?
RFM code = (R)(F)(M) = (4)(3)(5) = 435
A 435 customer bought recently (R=4) and is a top spender (M=5) — a high-value, low-frequency target for cross-sell campaigns.
NP2An A/B test runs each variant on 12,000 visitors. Variant A (control) gets 480 conversions; Variant B gets 612. What is the relative lift of B over A, in %?  Lift = (B−A)/A × 100 on conversions.
Conv A = 480/12,000 = 4.0% Conv B = 612/12,000 = 5.1% Relative lift = (B − A) / A × 100 = (612 − 480) / 480 × 100 = 132 / 480 × 100 = 27.5%
Because the sample sizes are equal, the lift on counts equals the lift on rates. B improves conversions by 27.5% over A.
NP3An AIDA funnel starts with 50,000 people in the Attention stage. By the Action stage only 1,500 complete a purchase. What is the overall funnel conversion rate (%)?
Overall conversion = (Action / Attention) × 100 = (1,500 / 50,000) × 100 = 3%
Only 3 in 100 who notice the campaign reach Action — typical of a top-of-funnel awareness push.
NP4In the same funnel, the Interest stage holds 18,000 users and the next stage (Desire) holds 7,200. What percentage of Interest-stage users are retained into Desire?
Retention = (Desire / Interest) × 100 = (7,200 / 18,000) × 100 = 40%
The drop-off here is 60% — the biggest leak in this funnel and the priority for optimisation.
NP5A campaign lifts monthly revenue from ₹240,000 to ₹312,000. What is the percentage change in revenue?
% change = (New − Old) / Old × 100 = (312,000 − 240,000) / 240,000 × 100 = 72,000 / 240,000 × 100 = 30%
NP6A brand sells ₹3.6 million in a category whose total market size is ₹30 million. What is the brand's market share (%)?
Market share = (Brand sales / Total market) × 100 = (3.6 / 30) × 100 = 12%
NP7Customer Lifetime Value (simple model) = average purchase value × purchases per year × retention (years). A customer spends ₹850 per purchase, buys 4 times a year, and stays for 3 years. What is the CLV (in ₹)?
CLV = avg purchase × purchases/year × retention years = 850 × 4 × 3 = ₹10,200
This figure caps how much you can profitably spend to acquire and retain that customer.
NP8A hypothesis test on a campaign returns a p-value of 0.03 with significance level α = 0.05. What is the correct decision?
Decision rule: if p < α, reject H₀. Here 0.03 < 0.05, so we reject the null hypothesis and conclude the result is statistically significant.
NP9A team uses dashboards to report what happened last quarter, then builds a regression model to forecast next quarter's sales. These two activities map to which two levels of analytics, respectively?
The 4 levels are Descriptive (what happened), Diagnostic (why), Predictive (what will happen), Prescriptive (what to do). Reporting past results = Descriptive; forecasting with regression = Predictive.

Q1 · 2 marks A customer is scored on a 1–5 RFM scale: Recency = 5, Frequency = 2, Monetary = 4. The RFM code is the three scores concatenated in R-F-M order. What code does this customer receive?
The code is R then F then M, concatenated (not summed or reordered).
RFM = (R)(F)(M) = (5)(2)(4) = 524
This is a recent, high-spending but infrequent buyer — a strong cross-sell / loyalty target. (425 reverses the order; 11 wrongly sums the digits.)
Q2 · 2 marks An A/B email test sends each variant to 8,000 recipients. Control A gets 320 conversions; treatment B gets 400 conversions. What is the relative lift of B over A?
Equal sample sizes, so lift on counts equals lift on rates.
Rate A = 320/8,000 = 4.0% Rate B = 400/8,000 = 5.0% Relative lift = (B − A)/A × 100 = (5.0 − 4.0)/4.0 × 100 = 25%
1% is only the absolute (percentage-point) gap; 20% wrongly divides by B; 125% is the ratio B/A, not the lift.
Q3 · 2 marks A campaign funnel begins with 80,000 people aware of the brand; 2,400 of them ultimately purchase. What is the overall funnel conversion rate?
Overall conversion divides the final stage by the first stage.
Conversion = (Purchasers / Aware) × 100 = (2,400 / 80,000) × 100 = 3%
2.4% wrongly uses 100,000 as the base; 33.3% inverts the ratio; 30% misplaces the decimal.
Q4 · 2 marks A retailer builds a dashboard reporting last quarter's regional sales, then drills down to discover that a stock-out in Mumbai caused the dip, and finally runs an optimiser that recommends the exact reorder quantity to maximise margin. These three activities map, in order, to which analytics levels?
Reporting what happened = Descriptive; finding the root cause (why) = Diagnostic; recommending the optimal action = Prescriptive. No forecasting of a future value occurs here, so Predictive does not appear.
Q5 · 2 marks A services firm wants to formalise the tangible cues customers use to judge quality — store ambience, layout and packaging. Which element of the 7Ps marketing mix is this, and is it one of the original 4Ps or one of the extended 3Ps?
Tangible cues of quality (ambience, layout, packaging) are Physical Evidence, one of the three extended Ps added for services (People, Process, Physical Evidence). The original 4Ps are Product, Price, Place, Promotion. Process is how the service is delivered, not the tangible cues.
Q6 · 2 marks An analyst maps a coffee-shop market with Porter's Five Forces. Coffee beans are available from countless suppliers, while customers can easily switch to tea, energy drinks or home brewing. Which two forces are being described, respectively?
Many interchangeable bean suppliers means each supplier has little leverage → Supplier Power is low. Easy alternatives like tea or home brewing → Threat of Substitutes is high. These are two of the five forces (the others being Rivalry, New Entrants, and Buyer Power).
